# Frontend Design Skill Summary

This document outlines an approach to creating distinctive, intentional visual design. Key principles include:

**Foundation**: Ground design in the subject matter itself—its materials, vernacular, and real content rather than templated defaults.

**Core Design Principles**:
- "Open with the most characteristic thing in the subject's world, in whatever form makes sense"
- Typography should carry personality through deliberate pairing and clear type scales
- Structure encodes information meaningfully, not decoratively
- Motion serves the subject with orchestrated moments rather than scattered effects
- Complexity should match the vision's ambition

**Process**: Work in two passes—first brainstorm a compact token system (color, type, layout, signature element), then critique against generic defaults before building code.

**Key Caution**: Avoid three common AI-generated defaults: warm cream backgrounds with serif + terracotta; near-black with acid-green/vermilion; or broadsheet layouts with hairline rules.

**Restraint**: "Spend your boldness in one place" and remove unnecessary decoration. Quality fundamentals (responsive design, keyboard focus, motion respect) should be built in without announcement.

**Writing Approach**: Copy serves clarity and usability. Write from the user's perspective using active voice, specific language, and conversational tone matched to audience and brand.
